# Project Rubrics

## Rubrics: Report

### Context & Purpose
* Refers to overall quality of the paper
* Level of awareness of context, and purpose of the assigned task
* How well the written task’s requirements are addressed

### Content Development
* Refers to mastery of the subject
* Clear focus
* Arguments/ideas that are well-developed with evidence/supporting materials
* A clear conclusion that highlights the key arguments/ideas and recommendations

### Organisation of Content
* Refers to the way in which content is structured
* Clear organisational pattern and structure
* Logical sequencing of ideas

### Language Use
* Refers to the fluency and accuracy of language used to communicate ideas
* Well-constructed sentences and appropriate choice of words that help to communicate ideas in a clear, coherent and concise manner
* Correct use of grammar, spelling, sentence/paragraph structure, punctuation and capitalisation

### Sources
* Refers to the use of sources to support key claims
* How well the sources are integrated within the paper’s argument
* Relevance, reliability and accuracy of the sources, including appropriate attribution and use of quotations
* Variety of sources used

### Citation
* Refers to the formatting conventions adopted for citing the sources
* The citation of evidence according to the appropriate format

## Rubrics: Presentation

### Inquiry by Discerning and Interpreting Information
*(Ability to seek out, discern, and interpret relevant information and evidence)*
* **Information gathering for problem understanding:** ability to gather information and find relevant facts to further understand a problem.
* **Interpretation and use of evidence:** Ability to select, interpret and effectively use evidence.

### Central Message and Content Development
*(Goal of the presentation)*
* Clarity of central message and arguments raised.
* Relevance of central message and arguments raised.
* Development of central message and arguments raised.

### Content Organisation
*(How content is structured to help audience understand)*
* Logical ordering of ideas
* Coherence, unity, transitions

### Delivery
*(Aspects of the presentation style that either enhance or interfere with the audience’s understanding)*
* **Verbal strategies:** vocal variety (volume, pace, pitch), pronunciation, enunciation
* **Non-verbal strategies:** posture, stance, facial expressions, gestures, eye contact

### Visual Aids
*(Use of slides, prototypes, etc. to support the principal ideas of the presentation)*
* Suitability of visual aids
* Design elements and accessibility
* Typography (font size and type)
* Colour scheme
* Layout of information
* Graphics
